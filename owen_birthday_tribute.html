Happy Birthday,
Owen Otto

songwriter · guitarist · creator · performer · friend · dad · dreamer of beautiful things

"Digging through archives and scattered photos
These sepia bones
The only thing that feels alive"
— Sepia Bones

For over three decades, Owen Otto has been doing what the rarest humans do — turning the quiet ache of being alive into something everyone can hear. From backyard shows in Petaluma, Berkeley, and San Francisco, to playing psychedelic cumbia with Cumbia Paradiso, to solo apartment recordings in Switzerland, he has never stopped building his mini-symphonies out of the stories of ordinary and extraordinary days. He is a songwriter who finds the ghost in every room, the astronaut in every drift, the old friend in every stranger's biography. He is a guitarist who makes the instrument think out loud. He is a writer who knows that sepia bones are sometimes the only things that feel alive. And beyond all of it, he is a generous, hilarious, deeply creative human being — a friend who shows up, a dad who dazzles, a creator who keeps going.
